<span class="verse thi_42_1">  <sup>1</sup>As the deer pants for streams of water, <br/>   so my soul pants for you, my God. <br/></span>
<span class="verse thi_42_2">  <sup>2</sup>My soul thirsts for God, for the living God. <br/>   When can I go and meet with God? <br/></span>
<span class="verse thi_42_3">  <sup>3</sup>My tears have been my food <br/>   day and night, <br/>  while people say to me all day long, <br/>   “Where is your God?” <br/></span>
<span class="verse thi_42_4">  <sup>4</sup>These things I remember <br/>   as I pour out my soul: <br/>  how I used to go to the house of God <br/>   under the protection of the Mighty One<a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="See Septuagint and Syriac; the meaning of the Hebrew for this line is uncertain.">⚓</a><br/>  with shouts of joy and praise <br/>   among the festive throng. <br/><br/></span>
<span class="verse thi_42_5">  <sup>5</sup>Why, my soul, are you downcast? <br/>   Why so disturbed within me? <br/>  Put your hope in God, <br/>   for I will yet praise him, <br/>   my Savior and my God. <br/><br/></span>
<span class="verse thi_42_6">  <sup>6</sup>My soul is downcast within me; <br/>   therefore I will remember you <br/>  from the land of the Jordan, <br/>   the heights of Hermon—from Mount Mizar. <br/></span>
<span class="verse thi_42_7">  <sup>7</sup>Deep calls to deep <br/>   in the roar of your waterfalls; <br/>  all your waves and breakers <br/>   have swept over me. <br/><br/></span>
<span class="verse thi_42_8">  <sup>8</sup>By day the Lord directs his love, <br/>   at night his song is with me— <br/>   a prayer to the God of my life. <br/><br/></span>
<span class="verse thi_42_9">  <sup>9</sup>I say to God my Rock, <br/>   “Why have you forgotten me? <br/>  Why must I go about mourning, <br/>   oppressed by the enemy?” <br/></span>
<span class="verse thi_42_10">  <sup>10</sup>My bones suffer mortal agony <br/>   as my foes taunt me, <br/>  saying to me all day long, <br/>   “Where is your God?” <br/><br/></span>
